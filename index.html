Mandala Art

  • * Introduction to Mandala

    Mandala in Sanskrit means a "magic circle" which is represention of the universe in the form of circle. Mandala designs are in geometric form mainly circles contained within a square arranged into sections that starts from central point. Mandala art is a symbolism in Hindu and Buddhist cultures. Its blend of art forms in a therapeutic fashion.

    Dot mandala is originated from oboriginal Australia. Acrylic paints are used for Dot painting. Specific dotting tools are used for dot painting It can be done on any surface like, Wood, glass, bottles, mdf, Earthern pot etc.

Decoupage art

  • *Introduction to Decoupage

    Decoupage or 3D decoupage is mainly a paper art. It is used to decorate various type of objects. Colored papers (example - Tissue paper's, rice papers.etc.) are glued to give a new look to the objects. Objects can be anything like wood, glass, bottles, pots, plates. etc. Objects can be of any size, new or old (example - Furniture, etc)

    The origin of decoupage is thought to be East Siberian tomb art. Nomadic tribes used cut out felts to decorate the tombs of their deceased. From Siberia, the practice came to China, and by the 12th century, cut out paper was being used to decorate lanterns, windows, boxes and other objects.

Lippan Art

  • *Introduction to Lippan art

    Lippan art is the traditional clay art originated from Gujarat. It was mostly done by women in the Kutch area. In order to decorate and beautify their homes, women used use mud and cow dung to create motifs on the walls (both inside and outside) of their huts. It is also called clay relief work.

    These days Lippan art is also being done using clay(paper mesh, mouldit etc) on various surfaces like wood, plates, earthern pot, etc.

    The lippan art is mainly in white, mud color with mirror embedded work in it. People also make colorful lippan art to make it more attactive

Glass Painting

  • * Introduction to Glass Painting

    Glass painting was introduced a way long back in twelth century in Gothic period.
    Reverse Glass Painting is one of the forms of glass painting. In reverse glass painting the image can be viewed only by turning over the other side of the glass Glass is used as a base in glass painting as we use canvas in other art forms. We can use OHP sheets instead of glass
    Glass painting was adopted by other countries and were renovated according to their ideologies. In India, glass painting reflected symbolic and cultural attributes.

Liquid Pour art

  • * Introduction to Liquid Pour art

    Pour art was discovered by accident in the 1930s by a famous muralist by the name of David Alfaro Siqueiros Pouring seems to be trendy in the art world these days
